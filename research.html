Active · LIGO / SkAI Institute Advisor: Prof. Michael Coughlin

Anomaly Detection for Astronomical Transients

Developing an evidential deep learning Transformer with a Dirichlet output head to classify ZTF Bright Transient Survey sources and detect anomalies — using tidal disruption events (TDEs) as the held-out anomaly class. Detection relies on trajectory-based Mahalanobis distance across observation horizons, with uncertainty features including vacuity, tr(Cov), total entropy, aleatoric entropy, and mutual information.

Contributed to the TDAbench 2026 workshop (SkAI Institute, Chicago), building a benchmark dataset with WISE/Gaia/TESS crossmatches via LSDB, Fausnaugh TESS photometry integration, and joint ZTF+TESS light curve construction.
