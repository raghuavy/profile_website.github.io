Anomaly Detection for Astronomical Transients

Developing an evidential deep learning Transformer with a Dirichlet output head to classify ZTF Bright Transient Survey sources and detect out-of-distribution anomalies, using tidal disruption events (TDEs) as the held-out anomaly class.

Detection is based on trajectory-level Mahalanobis distance across observation horizons, with uncertainty features including vacuity, tr(Cov), total entropy, aleatoric entropy, and mutual information. Contributed to the TDAbench 2026 workshop (SkAI Institute, Chicago), building a benchmark dataset with WISE/Gaia/TESS crossmatches via LSDB and joint ZTF+TESS light curve construction.
